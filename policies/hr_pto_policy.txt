EMPLOYEE LEAVE & PTO POLICY
Version: 2.4 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Department: Human Resources

---

SECTION 1: PTO ACCRUAL AND ELIGIBILITY

1.1 Full-Time Employees
Full-time employees accrue PTO at the following rates based on tenure:
  - 0–2 years of service: 15 days (120 hours) per year
  - 2–5 years of service: 18 days (144 hours) per year
  - 5+ years of service: 22 days (176 hours) per year

PTO accrues on a per-pay-period basis and is available for use as it accrues. Employees may not use PTO in advance of accrual without written manager approval.

1.2 Part-Time Employees
Part-time employees accrue PTO on a prorated basis relative to their scheduled hours. Employees working fewer than 20 hours per week are not eligible for PTO accrual.

1.3 Eligibility Period
New full-time employees are eligible to begin accruing PTO on their first day of employment. PTO may be used after 90 days of continuous employment.

---

SECTION 2: REQUESTING TIME OFF

2.1 Advance Notice Requirements
  - Planned time off (vacation, personal days): Minimum 5 business days' advance notice required.
  - Extended leave (5+ consecutive business days): Minimum 10 business days' advance notice required.
  - Emergency or unplanned absences: Notify your direct manager or HR as soon as reasonably possible.

2.2 Approval Process
All PTO requests must be submitted through the HR portal. Requests are subject to manager approval based on business needs and team coverage. Approval is not guaranteed and may be denied during high-demand periods or staffing constraints.

2.3 Blackout Periods
Certain periods may be designated as PTO blackout periods due to business-critical events (e.g., fiscal year-end, product launches, peak operational periods). Employees will be notified of blackout periods with at least 30 days' advance notice.

---

SECTION 3: SICK LEAVE

3.1 Sick Leave Entitlement
All employees receive 10 days (80 hours) of sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not roll over to the following year and is separate from PTO accrual.

3.2 Documentation Requirements
  - Absences of 3 or more consecutive days: A medical certificate or healthcare provider note is required upon return.
  - Recurring absences: HR may request documentation for repeated short absences if a pattern is identified.

3.3 Family Care
Employees may use up to 5 sick days per year to care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent) with a qualifying illness or medical appointment.

---

SECTION 4: LEAVE OF ABSENCE

4.1 Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)
Employees who have worked for the company for at least 12 months and 1,250 hours are eligible for up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act for qualifying reasons. Contact HR directly to initiate an FMLA request.

4.2 Parental Leave
  - Primary caregiver: 12 weeks of paid parental leave following birth, adoption, or foster placement.
  - Secondary caregiver: 4 weeks of paid parental leave.
  Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the qualifying event.

4.3 Bereavement Leave
  - Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 paid business days.
  - Extended family (grandparent, in-laws): 3 paid business days.
  - Other relationships: Up to 1 day at manager discretion.

---

SECTION 5: PTO CARRYOVER AND PAYOUT

5.1 Annual Carryover
Employees may carry over a maximum of 5 unused PTO days into the following calendar year. Balances exceeding this cap are forfeited on January 1 of the new year.

5.2 PTO Payout on Separation
Upon voluntary resignation or termination, accrued and unused PTO is paid out at the employee's current hourly rate, subject to applicable state laws. PTO is not paid out for employees terminated for cause unless required by law.

---

SECTION 6: HOLIDAYS

The company observes 11 paid holidays per calendar year. The official holiday schedule is published by HR in December for the following year. Employees required to work on a company holiday receive compensatory time off to be taken within 90 days.
