EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT & BUSINESS TRAVEL POLICY
Version: 3.1 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Department: Finance & Operations

---

SECTION 1: GENERAL REIMBURSEMENT PRINCIPLES

1.1 Eligible Expenses
The company reimburses employees for reasonable, necessary, and pre-approved business expenses incurred in the performance of their job duties. All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date. Expenses submitted after 60 days may be denied.

1.2 Approval Requirements by Amount
  - Under $50: Submit via expense portal; manager approval occurs automatically upon submission.
  - $50–$250: Direct manager approval required before or within 5 business days of the expense.
  - $251–$1,000: Manager approval plus department head sign-off required.
  - Over $1,000: Pre-approval from VP-level or above required prior to incurring the expense.

1.3 Documentation Requirements
Receipts are required for all expenses over $25. Receipts must show vendor name, date, itemized charges, and total amount. Credit card statements alone are not acceptable substitutes for itemized receipts.

---

SECTION 2: MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT

2.1 Business Meals — Employees Only
Meals taken alone during business travel are reimbursed at a per diem rate of $65/day (breakfast: $15, lunch: $20, dinner: $30). Alcohol purchased solely for personal consumption is not reimbursable.

2.2 Client and Prospect Meals
Meals with clients, prospects, or external partners may be reimbursed when there is a legitimate business purpose. The expense report must include:
  - Names and business titles of all attendees
  - Business purpose of the meeting
  - Total cost (excluding gratuity over 20%)

Client meal expenses up to $150 per person require manager approval. Expenses exceeding $150 per person require VP-level pre-approval.

2.3 Team Meals and Events
Team lunches or morale events require manager pre-approval and are subject to a $40 per-person budget. Events exceeding this threshold require department head approval.

2.4 Alcohol Policy for Business Entertainment
Alcohol is reimbursable for client entertainment when incidental to a business meal. Standalone bar tabs or alcohol-only receipts are not reimbursable. Total alcohol charges should not exceed 25% of the total meal bill.

---

SECTION 3: BUSINESS TRAVEL

3.1 Air Travel
  - Domestic flights under 4 hours: Economy class only.
  - Domestic flights over 4 hours or international flights: Economy Plus or Premium Economy permitted with manager pre-approval; business class requires VP approval.
  - Flights must be booked through the company's designated travel portal unless it is unavailable, in which case the most economical available option must be chosen.
  - Advance booking of at least 14 days is expected where possible to minimize cost.

3.2 Hotel Accommodation
  - Domestic U.S. (major cities): Up to $250/night before taxes.
  - Domestic U.S. (non-major cities): Up to $175/night before taxes.
  - International: Up to $300/night before taxes, or standard market rate with manager approval.
  - Lodging upgrades beyond these caps require pre-approval. Personal charges (room service, minibar, pay-per-view) are not reimbursable.

3.3 Ground Transportation
  - Rental cars must be booked through the company portal. Employees must decline the rental agency's collision damage waiver (CDW); the company carries a blanket policy.
  - Taxis, rideshare (Uber, Lyft): Reimbursable for business-related travel. Personal detours are not covered.
  - Personal vehicle use: Reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate. Commute mileage is excluded.
  - Parking: Reasonable parking fees are reimbursable with receipts.

3.4 Travel Advance Requests
Employees expecting out-of-pocket expenses over $500 may request a travel advance by submitting a Travel Advance Request form at least 5 business days before departure. Advances must be reconciled within 10 business days of return.

---

SECTION 4: NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES

The following are not eligible for reimbursement under any circumstances:
  - Personal entertainment (movies, sporting events, gym fees) unless part of an approved client event
  - Traffic or parking violations
  - Lost or stolen personal items
  - Expenses for a spouse or family member unless specifically pre-approved as a business guest
  - Upgrades purchased without prior approval
  - Expenses outside the submission window (over 60 days)

---

SECTION 5: EXPENSE REPORT SUBMISSION

Expense reports must be submitted via the company expense portal with all receipts attached as scanned images or photos. Reports submitted on paper will not be processed. Approved expenses are reimbursed within 10 business days via direct deposit to the employee's payroll account.
