PERSONAL INFORMATION & DATA PRIVACY POLICY
Version: 2.1 | Effective Date: March 15, 2024
Department: Legal & Compliance

---

SECTION 1: DEFINITION OF PERSONALLY IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION (PII)

Personally Identifiable Information (PII) refers to any data that can be used to identify, contact, or locate a specific individual. The company recognizes two categories of PII:

1.1 Sensitive PII (Highest Protection Required)
The following data elements are classified as Sensitive PII and require the highest level of protection:
  - Social Security Numbers (SSN) or National Identification Numbers
  - Passport numbers and government-issued ID numbers
  - Driver's license numbers
  - Financial account numbers (bank accounts, credit/debit card numbers)
  - Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition data, retinal scans)
  - Medical or health information (diagnoses, prescriptions, treatment records)
  - Genetic information
  - Authentication credentials (passwords, PINs, security questions/answers)
  - Immigration status or citizenship documentation

1.2 Standard PII (Elevated Protection Required)
  - Full legal name combined with any other identifier
  - Home address or mailing address
  - Email address
  - Phone numbers (mobile or home)
  - Date of birth
  - IP addresses associated with an individual
  - Device identifiers linked to a person
  - Location data (GPS, physical location records)
  - Racial or ethnic origin
  - Political opinions or religious beliefs

SECTION 2: PROHIBITED DATA HANDLING PRACTICES

2.1 Support Ticket Restrictions
Customer support tickets must NOT contain the following:
  - Full Social Security Numbers (SSNs) — even partial SSNs (last 4 digits) are only acceptable when necessary for identity verification and must be removed from ticket records within 30 days.
  - Full credit card or bank account numbers
  - Passwords or authentication credentials of any kind
  - Unencrypted medical records or diagnoses

Agents who receive messages containing SSNs or financial account numbers must:
  1. Immediately notify the customer NOT to share such information via ticket
  2. Delete or redact the data from the ticket using the [REDACT] tool in the CRM
  3. Log the incident using the Data Incident Report form within 4 hours

2.2 Storage Restrictions
Sensitive PII must not be stored in:
  - Plain text files or unencrypted databases
  - Email bodies or email attachments
  - Chat logs or internal messaging platforms
  - Support tickets or CRM notes (unless explicitly required and secured)
  - Shared drives or public-facing storage systems

---

SECTION 3: DATA ACCESS CONTROLS

Access to PII data is strictly role-based:
  - Tier 1 Support: May view name, email, phone, and account ID only
  - Tier 2 Support: May view all Standard PII with business justification
  - Compliance Officers: May access Sensitive PII with documented purpose
  - IT Security: Full access for audit and incident response only

Sharing PII with third parties requires a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) on file. Sharing without a DPA is a compliance violation.

---

SECTION 4: DATA BREACH PROTOCOL

In the event of a suspected PII data breach:
  1. Immediately notify the Security Operations team (security@company.com or ext. 5500)
  2. Do not attempt to investigate or contain the breach independently
  3. Preserve all logs and do not delete or modify any records
  4. The Security team will engage the Incident Response Plan within 1 hour

Regulatory notification requirements:
  - GDPR: Supervisory authority must be notified within 72 hours
  - CCPA: Affected individuals notified without unreasonable delay
  - HIPAA (if applicable): HHS notification within 60 days

---

SECTION 5: EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES

All employees handling PII must:
  - Complete annual PII and Data Privacy Training (mandatory)
  - Never share PII via personal devices or personal email accounts
  - Lock workstations when leaving desk (even briefly)
  - Report suspicious requests for PII to the Privacy Officer immediately

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may result in personal civil or criminal liability.
