CASE ESCALATION & COMPLIANCE REFERRAL POLICY
Version: 1.8 | Effective Date: February 1, 2024
Department: Customer Operations & Compliance

---

SECTION 1: OVERVIEW AND PURPOSE

This policy defines when and how customer cases must be escalated within the support organization, and when cases must be referred to the Compliance department. Proper escalation ensures that high-risk, complex, or legally sensitive situations are handled by the appropriate personnel.

---

SECTION 2: STANDARD ESCALATION TRIGGERS (TIER 1 → TIER 2)

A Tier 1 Support agent must escalate a case to Tier 2 when any of the following conditions are met:
  - The customer has contacted support 3 or more times for the same unresolved issue
  - The issue involves a technical failure affecting more than one account
  - The customer explicitly requests to speak with a supervisor or manager
  - The resolution requires system-level access beyond Tier 1 permissions
  - The case involves a billing dispute greater than $200

---

SECTION 3: ESCALATION TO CUSTOMER OPERATIONS MANAGEMENT

Tier 2 agents must escalate to a Customer Operations Manager when:
  - A refund or credit request exceeds $500 (per Refund Policy Section 1.3)
  - The customer threatens legal action, chargebacks, or regulatory complaints
  - The case has been open for more than 7 business days without resolution
  - A Tier 2 agent is uncertain how to proceed and no other senior agent is available
  - The issue involves a VIP or enterprise account

---

SECTION 4: MANDATORY COMPLIANCE ESCALATION

The following situations REQUIRE immediate escalation to the Compliance Department. Agents must not attempt to resolve these independently:

4.1 Legal and Regulatory Triggers
  - Any customer or third party claims a legal right (e.g., GDPR Subject Access Request, CCPA deletion request, court order, subpoena)
  - Any request from law enforcement or government agency for customer data
  - A customer alleges discrimination, ADA accommodation issues, or civil rights violations

4.2 Data and Privacy Triggers
  - A suspected or confirmed data breach or unauthorized PII disclosure
  - A customer reports receiving another customer's information
  - An agent discovers that Sensitive PII (SSN, financial account numbers) was stored or shared improperly
  - A third-party vendor reports a security incident affecting company data

4.3 Financial Crime Triggers
  - Suspected fraud, money laundering, or identity theft
  - Unusual patterns of refund requests from the same customer or account cluster
  - Customer account flagged by Trust & Safety team

4.4 Reputational or Media Risk
  - A customer identifies themselves as a journalist, regulator, or public figure
  - A case is related to an active litigation or known regulatory investigation
  - Social media escalation involving public complaints tied to a specific case

---

SECTION 5: HOW TO ESCALATE TO COMPLIANCE

Step 1: Do not make any commitments or statements to the customer about the outcome.
Step 2: Inform the customer that their case is being reviewed by a specialized team and they will receive an update within 2 business days.
Step 3: Submit a Compliance Referral Form via the internal compliance portal (compliance.intranet/referral).
Step 4: Attach all relevant case notes, ticket history, and supporting documentation.
Step 5: Send a notification email to compliance-escalations@company.com with the ticket number and referral category.
Step 6: Do NOT close the ticket. Mark it as "Pending Compliance Review."

---

SECTION 6: ESCALATION TIMELINES

  - Standard escalation (Tier 1 → Tier 2): Within current shift or 4 hours maximum
  - Management escalation: Within 2 business hours of trigger event
  - Compliance escalation: Immediately upon identification — same shift, no exceptions
  - Trust & Safety escalation: Immediately, with phone call backup if portal is unavailable

---

SECTION 7: DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

All escalations must be documented in the CRM with:
  - Escalation reason (selected from approved category list)
  - Timestamp of escalation decision
  - Agent employee ID
  - Name of receiving team or individual (if known)
  - Summary of steps taken before escalation

Failure to document an escalation is itself a policy violation, regardless of whether the escalation was handled correctly otherwise.

---

SECTION 8: AGENT PROTECTION

Agents who escalate in good faith based on this policy are protected from disciplinary action even if the escalation later proves unnecessary. If you are uncertain whether a situation requires compliance escalation, escalate. It is always safer to escalate than to handle a compliance-sensitive matter independently.
